When you're playing a harmonic, any harmonic, you are actually playing playing a string at its perfect division. At its perfect ratio so to say.

Take for example the natural harmonics, the ones you play over the 12th fret. The area where you place your finger for that harmonic is actually the perfect / near perfect half of the whole string.

You can use the same example for the 5th and 7th fret harmonics since they divide the string into 3rds and 4ths respectfully.

We'll call these perfect divisions "Nodes". So if you're dividing a string in half, you'll have one node aka the natural harmonic on the 12th fret. By 3rds? The node will be on the 5th fret. You get the picture.

Pinch harmonics works in a simliar way; instead of going the full string distance (Nut to Bridge), you'll be measuring from a variable distance (Finger to bridge) AND pluck that harmonic.

For example, if you were on the placing your finger on the 9th fret, you'll be measuring the distance from your finger to the bridge; making the node for the HALF the length of the string around the area where the 20th fret is located.
